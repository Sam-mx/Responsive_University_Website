UOS BUILD Program

Living in a globally connected world requires a global mindset and an awareness of your identity as a global citizen. UOS BUILD is a global leadership program designed to harness your leadership potential and develop your competencies as a global citizen and leader equipping you to deliver positive impact within greater society and the world.
About the program
UOS BUILD (Beyond UOS International Leadership Development) is an extra-curricular global leadership program preparing you to become global leaders and active global citizens.
The program is designed with our values in mind - Leadership, Global Citizenship, Empowerment, Intercultural Competency, Diversity and Inclusion and Social and Environmental Sustainability.
BUILD Values

LEADERSHIP

Enabling students to leverage their potential to deliver positive influence in a complex world, by equipping them with the skillset and qualities that underpin effective leadership, such as self-awareness, empathy, integrity, respect, resilience, critical thinking, decision-making, collaboration, and communication.

GLOBAL CITIZENSHIP

Fostering students' comprehension of global connectedness and the associated responsibility to make meaningful contributions towards the collective good of people and planet.

EMPOWERMENT

Equipping students with information, skills and confidence to challenge the status quo, seek solutions to global challenges, pursue individual and collective action that delivers positive impact in society and the world.

INTERCULTURAL COMPETENCY, DIVERSITY AND INCLUSION

Building students' skills to engage, communicate, and collaborate effectively with people from diverse backgrounds, beliefs, and orientations, embracing cultural differences, and promoting mutual support to build inclusive communities.

SOCIAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SUSTAINABILITY

Fostering awareness and empowering students to address social and environmental challenges, promoting a sense of collective agency for ongoing, meaningful contribution to society.
Program Summary
We have many opportunities that help you explore global issues, develop a global mindset, and build the professional skills you need for a truly international career that delivers positive social impact in the world.
You can curate your program experience by joining interactive workshops, learn from inspiring speakers about global issues and ideas, participate in experiential activities, explore endorsed online courses that support your understanding of important global issues, such as the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
You can also add one or more global immersive experiences such as a six-month Global Exchange or 2 to 6 week Global Short Program
You’ll collect points with each endorsed activity and once you’ve collected 100 points, you can complete the BUILD program. Your special achievement will be mentioned on your Myanmar Higher Education Graduation Statement (MHEGS) when you graduate from UOS. You will also attend a Completion Celebration and be presented with your BUILD Completion Certificate.
Planning your leadership journey
BUILD delivers and endorses a variety of events, workshops, online courses and experiential activities, to help you achieve your core and elective points. The content address three broad areas:
  • Individual identity as a global citizen (your attitude, values and skill set)
  • Ideas and issues affecting our global and local community; and
  • Implementing learning to deliver positive social impact in the world
To complete the BUILD Program, you need to achieve a minimum of:
  • 40 Core points via core activities
  • 60 Elective points via events, online courses and experiential activities
  • Personal reflection task
Once you have completed your reflection, you will be invited to the next upcoming Completion Celebration held twice per year, once at the end of each main academic session. You will then receive your BUILD Completion Certificate and the BUILD Program will be recognised on your Myanmar Higher Education Graduation Statement (MHEGS).
Core Workshops
BUILD core activities provide the foundation for you to develop the awareness, skills and knowledge that underpins your global leadership journey. The topics explore your identity as a leader and global citizen and help you develop the skills and mindsets required to make meaningful contributions to society.
Electives
BUILD electives include events, online courses and experiential activities designed to complement your foundational Core knowledge. Some of these are delivered by the BUILD Program and others are opportunities endorsed by BUILD and delivered in collaboration with, or by internal UOS or external organisations.
BUILD is designed to be thought-provoking, educational and practical and the suite of electives cover all of these areas. You can choose which electives suit your interests and your leadership journey.
Connect with us via admission.
Tracking your program
You can track your progress through the BUILD program on your Indeed dashboard. It can take 2-3 days for your submitted electives or your event attendance to be reviewed, approved and uploaded.
Via the dashboard, you will be able to view your cumulative points tally across core and elective experiences and know when you are close to 100 points.
We recommend planning your program over one year, or you may choose to spread it out over the duration of your UOS course.
Completing your program
Once you have achieved the program requirements, CareerHub will invite you to complete your overall BUILD Program Reflection and consider how you have changed over your BUILD global leadership journey. The reflection helps you review your identity as a global citizen and assess the skills and competences you have developed towards becoming a global leader. The reflection is a valuable step for BUILD to help us assess the impact of the program.
Once you have completed your reflection, you will be invited to the next Completion Celebration. These are held twice per year, once at the end of each main academic session. You will receive your BUILD Completion Certificate and the BUILD Program will be recognised on your Myanmar Higher Education Graduation Statement (MHEGS).
Can I continue in the BUILD program after completion?
You are welcome to continue attending BUILD events where we have sufficient capacity. As each event earns BUILD points, we preference students who are still seeking to earn points towards their overall 100. There may be BUILD on-campus volunteer opportunities you can support or other BUILD endorsed events you can attend.
Your global citizenship and leadership learning does not end when you complete the BUILD Program. It is a life long journey of engaging in a globalised world and we encourage you to continue seeking out your own opportunities and experiences to further your personal development.
Join BUILD to:
  • Develop a global mindset
  • Be inspired by people who are achieving great things globally
  • Create networks with your community of peers and professionals from diverse backgrounds
The program is free and open to all UOS students from any academic discipline regardless of whether you are an undergraduate or postgraduate, international or domestic student.

Accessibility Service

The Accessibility Service is the University’s central contact point for all students living with one or more disabilities, medical or mental health conditions.
About the Accessibility Service
The Accessibility Service is the University’s central contact point for all students living with one or more disabilities, medical or mental health conditions. The Accessibility Service provides assistance and support to access services, and to make requests for assessment arrangements and reasonable adjustments.
The main purpose of the Accessibility Service is to enable students' participation in their learning and coursework. Reasonable adjustments aim to minimise the impacts of disability/health conditions upon study as far as possible, whilst maintaining academic integrity.
While the main focus remains on student's course progression, the Accessibility Service encourages students to take up opportunities for:
  • building upon their individual strengths and strategies
  • learning how to manage their disabilities/health conditions in the study environment
  • preparing for transition following university.
The Accessibility Service assists students to develop their independence, self-determination and self-advocacy skills to enable participation in a productive and concerted way.

Why update my accessibility details?

Updating your accessibility details on My Student Admin (MSA) helps to ensure UTS is providing accurate data to the government. The funding model is based upon the number of students who share their accessibility information on MSA, rather than the number of students who are registered with the Accessibility Service (a student may wish to share their accessibility details on MSA but decide not to register with the Service, or vice versa). The data collected on MSA is the mechanism the government uses to determine the number of students with a disability across universities.

What happens when I update my details on My Student Admin?

The information sent to the government is de-identified so the specific details of your accessibility information is confidential to uos. The information you share is not available to uos general administrative staff — for example, the Student Centre; uos students; faculty staff; or general academic staff. The information is not available or shown on your uos transcript or Canvas.

Important note

The Accessibility Service is made available to service users subject to demand, which varies at different times in the academic year. Priority for earlier appointments is given to students who acquire a disability after commencing study. Students with known disabilities are expected to make contact as early as possible to request assistance. Service users will be informed of the nature, frequency and duration of service that is reasonable, based on the level of demand, staff availability, and service user needs.
The University reserves the right to take all necessary action to protect the safety of its staff, students and facilities, and to ensure reasonable use of services and facilities. This may include revoking or placing conditions on access to facilities, services, or campus precincts.
Services and assistance
Services and assistance We offer a range of services and facilitate requests for reasonable adjustments for participation and assessment for students that are registered with the Accessibility Service.
Our team of Accessibility Consultants (ACs) will consult with you about your needs, and with your agreement, they can help you in a number of ways, including:
  • Confidential appointments where you can discuss how the University can provide you with an accessible learning environment to maximise your participation and minimise the impact of disability or health conditions on your studies.
  • Providing services to assist with access. These include both individual support services and general access assistance.
  • Consulting with you about your needs and making recommendations for learning and assessment adjustments for your Academic Liaison Officer (ALO) (PDF, 51kB). As part of your registration with the Accessibility Service and only with your consent, a referral of your learning and assessment adjustments will be made to your Faculty Academic Liaison Officer (ALO) who will work together with you and your AC.
  • Facilitating your application and recommending Exam Provisions if you will be disadvantaged by a disability /health condition in exams.
  • Developing a statement for your academic staff about the impact of your disability or ongoing health condition, with suggested inclusive practices to enable your participation.
  • Assisting you to link into relevant UOS services, as well as making referrals if needed.
To help us provide your support in a timely way:
  • Make an appointment to register with us early, i.e. as soon as you receive your offer, prior to the start of session, or following the a disability or health condition diagnosis that is impacting upon your study.
  • Contact us early if there’s something that we can help with.
  • Keep in touch and let us know how you’re going, and
  • Inform us about any problems as soon as they arise.

Academic Support

Course and subject advice
At UOS we want you to be successful in your studies – your success reflects on us as well. So if you're unsure whether the course or subjects you are doing are the right fit for you or if you are finding it hard going, there are people available to help you.

Getting help

For course and subject assistance related to:
  • academic issues and questions, speak with your lecturer or tutor initially, then subject or course co-ordinators. The most appropriate contacts will be in the Subject outline, which can be found on Canvas.
  • administrative issues and questions related to your course/subject and to find out who the relevant course/subject co-ordinator is, talk to someone in the Student Centre associated with your faculty.
  • preparing for transition following university.

Designated academic assistance roles

Academic advisers provide advice to assist students who are on:
  • academic caution
  • early intervention.
Academic liaison officers are academic staff in each Faculty who assist two groups of students:
  • with disabilities and ongoing illnesses;
  • who have difficulties in their studies because of their family commitments (e.g. being a primary carer for small children or a family member with a disability).
Responsible Academic Officers are senior academics in each Faculty who oversee assessment procedures and resolve conflicts relating to assessment. They are also responsible for the appointment of Academic Liaison Officers and for resolving any conflicts between ALOs and other academic staff in relation to assessment arrangements for students with disabilities.

HEALTH AND WELLBEING

Health service
The Health Service provides friendly and confidential medical services to students, staff, alumni and their families.
UOS Health Service is a QPA-accredited practice that has been supporting the community of UOS for many years. We aim to provide excellent healthcare in higher quality general practice setting to all UOS staff, students, alumni and their families. We offer face-to-face consultations as well as consultations via Telehealth (including video conferencing and telephone consultations).
Our computerised medical practice operates with doctors and registered nurses. Our caring and experienced staff offer a broad range of general practice services, including but not limited to:
  • General health and wellbeing
  • Women’s health
  • Men’s health
  • Sexual health
  • Travel medicine
  • Vaccinations for University placement compliance
  • Influenza vaccination, including government-supplied vaccines for vulnerable groups
  • Childhood vaccinations
We aim to promote continuity of care and to have an ongoing professional relationship with our patients. All requests for urgent appointments will be considered, and patients will be fitted in when required.
Douglass Hanly Moir Pathology provides pathology collection services on-site.
Hours of operation
The Health Service is open Monday to Friday throughout the year (except public holidays)

After-hours service and public holidays

* All patients who hold a current Medicare or Gold DVA card are bulk billed. Patients who do not have a valid Medicare or Gold Veteran Affairs card are charged a fee at the time of consultation. The fee is advised at the time of the booking. A receipt is issued for insurance claims where accepted.
Communicating with the practice
You can telephone the practice during business hours on 9514 1177. Doctors may be contacted by phone during normal practice hours. If the doctor is with a patient, a message will be taken and you will be advised when the doctor is likely to return your call. Email enquiries to the practice may be directed to health.service@uos.edu.au. Email communication will be actioned in 1–2 business days.
Important note
The Health Service is made available to UOS students, staff, alumni and their families. Medical practitioners determine whether and with what frequency they will accept patients for consultation. Services are generally provided via scheduled appointments. Patients may be prioritised in cases of medical emergency as determined by the triage process.
Consultations are scheduled for 15 minutes duration, to allow us to best serve all our patients.
If you have multiple or lengthy items to discuss, we ask could you please call our friendly reception staff to book subsequent appointments or book online—this will enable the doctor to optimally manage your health and our clinic to operate on time.
Please be on time for your appointment. Late arrivals will be managed at the clinic’s discretion, you may be asked to rebook your appointment, and a no-show fee may also be incurred.
