About Our Campus


The UOS Campus is a vibrant and diverse community that offers a range of facilities and resources to support your academic and personal growth. Here's an overview of what you can expect when you step onto our campus:

Campus Facilities

Our campus is equipped with state-of-the-art facilities, including modern classrooms, well-equipped laboratories, and extensive libraries to support your academic endeavors. We also offer dedicated spaces for research, innovation, and hands-on learning. More about our facilities

Green Spaces

UOS takes pride in its green and sustainable campus environment. You'll find lush gardens, outdoor seating areas, and green spaces that provide a peaceful backdrop for relaxation, study, and socializing.

Community and Inclusivity

We take pride in our diverse and inclusive campus community. UOS is a place where people from various backgrounds come together to learn, grow, and contribute to a global network of knowledge and understanding.

A vibrant and diverse community


Campus services at UOS

We have a range of services on campus, including various childcare options, security and several bars and cafes offering venue hire and catering.

Our student accommodation provides a balanced academic and social lifestyle in a supportive environment. Living among our vibrant community is one of the best life and educational experiences you can have as a student.

We're proud of our diversity on campus, and we promote a culture of support. Our initiatives support student diversity, including diversity in culture, language, gender, sexual orientation, age, ability, socio-economic status, and religion.

Your support network for success

Your first point of contact on campus for any queries


We are committed to providing an outstanding student contact experience: online, by phone or in person.

Service ConnectI is your first point of contact for enquiries about your life at UOS. We can assist as you navigate UOS's processes and services, helping point you in the right direction and making sure you have the resources you need.

    We can assist you with:

  • Enrolment and class registration support
  • Understanding the right policy or process to help you
  • Helping you find the information you need
  • Answering your questions about applications you might submit (eg. Recognition of Prior Learning, Course Transfer, Special Consideration)
  • Administration related to your studies at Macquarie (eg. Status Letters, Transcripts, changing your personal details)
  • Guidance you may require for your course progression or changes (eg. Course Guidance Form)

If your question or problem is IT related, contact the IT Service Desk.


Student Health


Student Health is a GP service for students currently enrolled at UOS

Book a same-day appointment; there’s no need to plan ahead.

Appointments are 15 minutes and suitable for one issue per appointment. Complex issues may require a follow-up appointment.

Student Health is available Monday to Friday, and is located at Suite 305, Level 3, 2 Technology Place, UOS.

For regular GP services, contact UOS Health General Practice on +95 9812 3944.

Course Guidance


Check your study goals

Use our course guidance to help you understand and plan your studies to make sure you're on the right path.

UOS has a range of resources available to help students understand their courses and make informed decisions about their studies. This self-help tool helps you find the resources you need to understand your course, its requirements, or the effects of making changes to your degree.

The Course Guidance form has three sections:

    Academic Standing
  • Learn about Academic Standing
  • Complete required actions to lift an Academic Standing sanction (for students on Monitored Enrolment or Probation)

    Course Progression Check
  • Learn how to determine your remaining units to plan your studies
  • Watch our video on how you can self-check your course progression
  • You should have completed at least half of your course prior to requesting a progression check. Review the information available in the form or view our video for guidance if you are early on in your course.

    Course Changes
  • Learn about Course Transfers (changing from one degree to another)
  • Learn how making changes to your Major, Minor, or Specialisation can affect your studies

Students in specific circumstances can also be referred for additional course support through this form.

Accommodation

Find a place to call home


Living on campus sets you up for success at university

Live in our Central Courtyard Accommodation, which is in the heart of the University campus, so there’s no excuse for missing those early lectures. Around the Central Courtyard Accommodation there’s the food precinct next door with dozens of cafes and restaurants to keep you entertained and the Sport and Aquatic Centre is a stone's throw away.

Enjoy the fun of communal living with a choice of Twin rooms, 4 Bedroom and 6 Bedroom apartment or have your own private apartment with our Studio and 1 Bedroom apartments, the choice is yours! Our accommodation offers the best on campus accommodation. There’s over 16 Common Rooms, Games Rooms, Study Rooms, TV Room, Two Laundry Rooms. We also have a full calendar of Residential Events and a dedicated on site Security Team 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Children and youth

UOS generations — children and youth


Macquarie University isn’t just for school leavers and graduates. We engage people of all ages — starting with innovative, inspiring activities for children and young people.

Whether you are looking for swimming lessons, school holiday programs, museums, sports, early learning centres or even a primary school, you’ve come to the right place!


Building foundations for life through education


We have three on-campus early learning centres providing high quality education and care for children aged six weeks to five years.

Our programs are child-focused, play-based and reflective of current research and the Australian Government, Department of Education's Early Years Learning Framework.

Each child's learning is facilitated, guided, and extended by our experienced team of educators. Our custom-designed indoor and outdoor environments are considered equally important in the provision of learning opportunities for children. Sustainability, inclusion, and diversity are embedded in our practices and policies. We believe:

  • relationships are formative to learning
  • every child should have access to high-quality education
  • innovation and creative thinking are integral to our educational programs.

Our centres

University qualified Early Childhood Teachers lead the program delivery under the guidance of the Educational Leader and supported by the Program and Practice Support Teacher. Our physical environments are designed to stimulate children's natural curiosity and wonder and we access University spaces and facilities, such as the Art Gallery and Library, to support and enhance our learning programs.

    Banksia and Gumnut Cottages

  • 80 permanent places per day across 5 playrooms
  • Children aged 6 weeks to 5 years
  • Open 7.30am to 6.30pm Monday to Friday

    Waratah Cottage

  • 47 permanent places per day across 3 playrooms
  • Children aged 6 weeks to 5 years
  • Open 7.30am to 6.30pm Monday to Friday

Campus Security

Ensuring you stay safe on campus


UOS prides itself on the security of its students, staff and campus.

Find out how we keep you safe on campus. Learn what to do in an emergency, how to access areas of campus relevant to your work or study, how to report incidents or threatening behaviour, and more.

Keeping the campus safe

We want you to feel secure on our campus. Familiarise yourself with our security services and learn how you can plan to keep yourself and your property safe.

Security response services

Our security response vehicle

Macquarie University security services are a highly visible presence in and around the campus precinct. Security staff patrol the University on foot, in marked all-wheel drive buggies and in a security response vehicle. The security vehicle is radio controlled, providing a fast response time to any situation.

    You will see our security vehicle:

  • patrolling the roadways of the campus
  • visiting the Macquarie University housing precincts
  • responding to alarms and other incidents around campus.

All our vehicles carry first aid equipment including an automatic external defibrillator and an Oxy-Reviver.

Master Licence: 407602396

Security services officers

Our friendly security staff strive to provide outstanding customer service wherever they are on campus. Security officers patrol all precincts 24 hours per day, seven days per week, and are able to assist with your enquiries.

All of our security officers are trained in first aid, and respond to incidents and events that impact the campus population, including fire alarms, injuries, thefts and reports of lost property.

Accessing security assistance

You can reach security services at various locations both during core business hours and after hours. Security are always present on campus.
