Scholarships and grants

Through scholarships, UOS endeavours to reward achievement and recognise motivation to succeed. UOS is also committed to providing support to students experiencing financial hardship and/or other educational disadvantages.
Undergraduate scholarships and grants
  • International Baccalaureate (IB) Scholarship - 50% tuition fees scholarship for the standard course duration.
  • Undergraduate Academic Excellence International Scholarship - 25% tuition fees scholarship for the duration.
  • Vice-Chancellor’s International Undergraduate Scholarship - Full tuition scholarship for the standard course duration.

Country and region-specific scholarships

  • Undergraduate Tuition Fee Scholarship - 20% off the standard course duration tuition fee.
Postgraduate coursework scholarships and grants
  • Grant for Masters by Coursework Students - $3,000 towards your first session tuition fees..
  • Postgraduate Academic Excellence International Scholarship - 30% off tuition fees for the standard course duration. (Available from 2024 intakes)
  • Vice-Chancellor’s International Postgraduate Coursework Scholarship – Full tuition scholarship for the standard course duration.

Course/faculty-specific scholarships

  • Postgraduate Business Scholarship - 20% off the standard course duration tuition fee.
  • Postgraduate International Scholarship - 20% off the standard course duration tuition fee.

Did you know?

Overview

This Scholarship was established by UOS to attract top achieving commencing international students who are completing the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma qualification, and are enrolling in an undergraduate program at UOS.

Who is eligible?

To be eligible to apply for the Scholarship, an applicant must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Must be an international student (not Myanmar Citizen); and
  • Commence full-time studies on campus. On campus studies include face-to-face and/or online (remote) learning applicable to all degrees except online degrees and distance mode degrees.; and
  • Must be completing or have completed an International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma no longer than 1 year prior to commencing at UOS, and be admitted based on this qualification to UOS; and
  • Meet the UOS admission criteria for the selected UOS undergraduate degree course; and
  • Must obtain an aggregate score including bonus and penalty points of 28 or above in their International Baccalaureate (IB) final grades, or 33 or above in their International Baccalaureate predicted grades at the point the scholarship decision is made; and
  • All candidates must apply using the UOS online Scholarships application form to be eligible; and

Must not:

  • be studying a UOS online degree or distance mode degree; or
  • be a government sponsored student; or
  • be a recipient of another UOS scholarship for the duration of this Scholarship.

On campus studies include face-to-face and/or online (remote) learning applicable to all degrees except online degrees and distance mode degrees.

Selection process

Academic merit based on International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma final results or International Baccalaureate predicted results.

Where there are multiple applicants with the same International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma results, further consideration will be given to the answer to the following questions:

  • Commitment to utilise your UOS degree to make a positive contribution your home society/country, as demonstrated by the Scholarship application; and
  • Personal experience and achievement to date, including but not limited to examples of community service, innovation or entrepreneurship.

Overview

To attract high performing commencing international students to enroll in an undergraduate coursework program at UOS.

Who is eligible?

To be eligible to apply for the Scholarship, an applicant must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Must be an international student (not Myanmar Citizen); and

Overview

This Scholarship was established to support high achieving commencing international students enrolling in an undergraduate program at UOS. The scholarship is to be offered to international students who exhibit the University’s core values and can demonstrate commitment to excellence, innovation, entrepreneurship and social justice.

Who is eligible?

To be eligible to apply for the Scholarship, an applicant must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Must be an international student (not Myanmar Citizen); and
  • meet all admission requirements for the selected course by the closing date of the scholarship application; and
  • Commence full-time studies on campus. On campus studies include face-to-face and/or online (remote) learning applicable to all degrees except online degrees and distance mode degrees.; and
  • have completed a Year 12 or high school studies comparable to a Year 12 oUOSide of Myanmar no longer than three (3) years prior to commencing at UOS and be admitted based on this qualification to UOS; and
  • have an overall average of 85% where the pass mark is 50% or equivalent in a UOS recognized high school qualification; and
  • All candidates must apply using the UOS online Scholarships application form to be eligible; and

Must not:

  • be studying a UOS online degree or distance mode degree; or
  • be a government sponsored student; or
  • be a recipient of another UOS scholarship for the duration of this Scholarship.

On campus studies include face-to-face and/or online (remote) learning applicable to all degrees except online degrees and distance mode degrees.

Selection process

The Scholarship will be awarded to the highest ranked applicant on the basis of academic merit in the academic qualification used as a basis for admission into the selected UOS Bachelor degree program coupled with the applicant’s response to the questions asked in the UOS online scholarship application.
